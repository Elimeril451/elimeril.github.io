I am a theoretical physicist and a PhD candidate at Tel Aviv University. I recently submitted my thesis focusing on Acoustoelectric Superlattices (ASL) and interlayer tunneling phenomena. My primary research explores the intersection of two-dimensional quantum phenomena, topological condensed matter physics, and moiré bands, working under the supervision of Wolf Prize laureate Prof. Rafi Bistritzer. 

**Research & Industry Experience**
Throughout my academic journey across Tel Aviv University and the Weizmann Institute of Science, I have engaged in diverse research areas, including quantum light-matter interaction, hydrodynamics, and scanning near-field optical microscopy (NSOM). Recently, I concluded my role as a Theoretical Physicist and Algorithm Developer in the CTO-R&D group at Nova Ltd., where I bridged theoretical concepts with practical algorithmic solutions. 

**Teaching & Problem Solving**
I am deeply passionate about physics education and tackling complex challenges. Having represented Israel at the International Physics Olympiad (APhO), I later served as an academic coach and lecturer for the national team. I continuously apply this problem-solving mindset to computational physics, utilizing my strong background in Python to build high-performance numerical simulations. 

When I'm not writing derivations or simulating physics, I enjoy exploring advanced mathematics puzzles and observing the night sky.
